Top Tips For Selling A Vehicle In Arizona

Selling a car can be a lot more stressful than it should be if you don’t take the right approach to doing it. There are literally dozens of mistakes that you can make as you sell a car that will send your stress levels skyrocketing.

Want to make sure that you’re able to steer clear of these mistakes so that you can enjoy a good experience when selling a car? We’ve created a list of tips for selling a car that should help.

Check out our tips for selling a vehicle in Arizona below and make sure you keep them in the back of your mind as you set out to sell a car.

Different Ways to Sell Your Automobile

The good news for those who are on the verge of selling a car is that there will be a variety of different ways in which you can do it. You should consider all of your available options before you decide which direction you’re going to go in when you sell a car in Arizona.

You can:

  • Trade your vehicle into a local dealership and use it to purchase a new car, truck, or SUV
  • Sell it yourself by placing a “For Sale” sign on it or, better yet, by listing it for sale online
  • Accept an instant cash offer from a company that specializes in paying cash for cars that people don’t want anymore

Generally speaking, selling your car yourself will usually give you an opportunity to make the most money. But you’ll need to learn how to sell a vehicle on your own before doing it, and you’ll also have to put in more work to sell a car in this way. So you should give some serious thought to which option will be the best one for you before you attempt to sell a vehicle in Arizona.

Documents to Gather Before Selling Your Vehicle

No matter how you choose to try and sell a car in Arizona, there are certain documents that you will need to obtain ahead of time. You might have a much harder time selling a car if you don’t have the right documents handy.

The title for your car will obviously be the most important document of the bunch. You will need to be able to produce an original title or, if you don’t have that, you’ll want to look into securing a duplicate title through one for the third-party MVD services in AZ.

You’ll also need to make sure you have these documents on hand when you’re trying to sell a vehicle:

  • Service records
  • Sales paperwork
  • CARFAX Vehicle History Report

If you don’t have any of these documents, it might make it more difficult for you to sell a car. It’s why you should visit a third-party DMV service in AZ to obtain the necessary documentation. This is one of the most important tips for selling a car on this entire list.

Getting Your Car Ready to Sell

If you sell your car as-is, you’re almost always going to get less money for it than you might like. To avoid this, you should prepare your car before you list it for sale.

To start, you should wash the exterior of your car from bumper to bumper to make it shine. You should also consider waxing it and possibly even having it detailed so that it looks a lot newer than it does now. It’ll make it more visually appealing to potential buyers.

Once you’re done doing that, you should turn your attention to the interior of your vehicle and clean that, too. You’ll want to vacuum out your car, shampoo any carpeting and/or upholstery in it, and wipe down every single surface with the appropriate car detailing cleaners.

The better your vehicle looks inside and out, the easier it will be for you to sell it fast for top dollar. You’ll be amazed by how much better you’ll be able to make it look by spending just a few hours cleaning every aspect of it.

How to Get the Value of Your Car

While you’re busy getting your car ready to sell, you should try to figure out what it might be worth right now. This will make it possible for you to come up with a price for it.

You can find out what your car might be worth by:

  • Seeing what other cars that are just like it are selling for online right now
  • Using Kelley Blue Book to get your car’s value
  • Asking cash car buyers to make you offers for your car

You should be able to get some sense of what your car is worth by using one or more of these methods. This way, you won’t have to worry about pricing your car too high and scaring buyers off or pricing it too low and leaving money on the table when you sell it.

Best Practices After Listing

Once you decide how much you would like to list your car for, you can go ahead and start trying to sell it. But a word to the wise: You need to be ready to spring into action when people start to show an interest in your car so that you don’t miss out on the chance to sell it.

First and foremost, you should begin responding to any inquiries that you get about your car as quickly as you can. You should also schedule test drives and/or inspections so that people can see whether or not they want to buy your car.

By taking these steps, you can ensure that you’re able to find an interested buyer and sell your car in no time at all.

Post-Sale Things to Remember

When you find someone willing to buy your car, you should spend some time negotiating with them so that you’re able to agree upon a price for it. From there, it’ll only be a matter of accepting payment from them and signing your car over to them so that they can drive away in it.

You should also make it a point to update your auto insurance policy after you sell a car. You can have this car removed from your policy so that you don’t continue to pay for insurance when you don’t need it anymore.
